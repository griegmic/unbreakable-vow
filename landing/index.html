The Top 3

Shreyas, Julie, and Nikita locked in a room. One question: "What is the best possible home screen for a first-time user who has 5 seconds of attention?"

THE ARGUMENT
Shreyas: "Before we pick layouts, let's agree on what's LOCKED. 'Make a vow. Mean it.' — locked. 'What commitment will you bet on?' — locked. 'I'm in' as the CTA — locked. Golden Hour brand — locked. The only open question is the 40 pixels between the hero and the input label. That's what we're designing."
Nikita: "Right. And that section needs to do ONE thing: stop the user from thinking 'I don't get it' long enough for them to type a vow. Once they type, they're in the funnel. The explainer's only job is to prevent the bounce."
Julie: "Agreed. But it has a SECOND job too — it needs to make them WANT to type. Comprehension without desire is a Wikipedia article. The best version does both: 'I get it AND I want to do it.'"
Shreyas: "So here's my framework. The user processes the screen top-down in 3 beats. Beat 1: 'Make a vow. Mean it.' — that's the IDENTITY. Beat 2: the explainer — that's the MECHANISM. Beat 3: 'What commitment will you bet on?' — that's the ACTION. Beat 2 bridges identity to action. It needs to be SHORT (≤3 lines) and it needs to answer: 'What happens?' Not 'why should I care' — the hero already did that."
Nikita: "OK so what are our three best versions of Beat 2? I think they're: prose that dares you, steps that teach you, and a line that trusts you. Three different confidence levels in the user."
