# Audit Synthesis — A vs B2

Four expert audits ran in parallel: copy, product, Gen-Z growth, visual density. They agree more than they disagree. Where they split, the split is informative.

---

## Where the auditors agree

1. **Both directions are shippable.** Nobody recommended killing either. The disagreement is about priority and which audience converts faster.
2. **The "Joey named you" sentence on the Witness Landing is the highest-leverage copy in the whole app.** Copy and product audits both flagged it. B2's variant — *"He's made a vow, and you're the one he trusts to call it kept or broken"* — was called "generational-level insight." Whichever direction wins, this line ships.
3. **Contract's witness CTA wins on intent, B2's wins on word choice.** "I'll witness it" honors the role; "Accept" is bureaucratic. Both audits agreed: the final witness CTA should be a 1–2 word active statement (`I'm in`, `I'll witness`, `I'm watching`).
4. **Kill "est. 2026" on B2.** Universal. Visual, copy, and Gen-Z audits all flagged it as ornament without function.
5. **Kill "A contract with yourself" eyebrow on A.** Copy and Gen-Z audits both flagged it. Replacement: *"A vow, made binding."*
6. **Both stake screens read as forms, not stores — keep them that way.** Product audit was emphatic: for money decisions users want frictionless inputs, not retail flourishes. Don't decorate the stake.
7. **Add a third destination option** ("cause they support" / "charity of their choice") to rebalance gender appeal. Currently the binary "love it / hate it" framing skews male.

|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Which direction wins overall?** | B2 (voice) | Hybrid (Monument stake + Contract witness) | **A ships today; B2 needs a pass** | B2 (cleaner type system, fewer levels) |

**The crux:** Gen-Z says A is the speed pick (instant recognition, screenshot bait, finance-TikTok-coded). B2 is the taste pick (broader gender appeal, premium voice, but slower to parse). A is more masculine-coded as built; B2 is more balanced.

## What this means for the v1/v2 builds

### A — Contract

**v1 (tight, ship-ready):** Strip every editorial flourish. Density at 24px sides, H1 48/28/28, line-height 1.4. Targets the Rocket Money / hustle-TikTok crowd: stake-first, fast.
- Eyebrow: `A vow, made binding.`
- Sub: parallel structure ("Tell a friend… Put money on it… Break it, money goes to charity — or a cause you hate.")
- Witness CTA: `I'm in`
- Cut: "A contract with yourself," "one Sunday from now," "Unbreakable Vow turns promises into contracts" footer
- Stake: keep tight grid, refund promise prominent, kill jargon ("seal" → "verdict")
- Add: third destination ("charity of their choice")

**v2 (loose, lifestyle):** Same backbone, more breathing room. Density at 32px sides, H1 60/36/32, line-height 1.6. Targets the Dimes Square / aesthetic-first crowd. Adds optional narrative element ("why I made this vow"). Witness page gets the full B2 "He's made a vow…" treatment.

### B2 — Monument

**v1 (tight, productivity):** Editorial structure but stripped of ornament. Density at 28px sides, H1 46/34/30, spacing unit 10px. Targets the Notion / Linear / intentional-living crowd.
- Cut: `Vol. I`, `est. 2026`, `Witness Edition`, `Summoned to witness`
- Replace witness dateline with: `Joey named you.` (borrow A's warmth)
- Sub: rewrite passive "it comes back" → active "you get it back"
- Cut: "No proof uploads, no debate" (two-clause filler)
- Keep: editorial grid, Newsreader for h1/quotes only, the *"He's made a vow, and you're the one he trusts to call it kept or broken"* line

**v2 (loose, literary):** Magazine breathing. Density at 36px sides, H1 56/42/38, line-height 1.6. Targets the Kinfolk / BookTok / wellness crowd. Keeps the Vol. I masthead with restraint, expands witness narrative, adds public vow wall hook.

## The cross-direction rules (from visual audit)

These hold for all 4 versions, regardless of family:

1. Serif used **only** for h1, pull-quotes, and large monetary values. Everything else is sans/mono.
2. Accent color used **only** for labels, borders, and the active CTA. Never for body text.
3. Headline line-height ≤ 1.1. Body ≥ 1.4.
4. Primary CTA height: 58–60px. Secondary actions are text-link or ghost button only.
5. Maximum 3 visual systems competing per screen (e.g., header band + body + CTA dock — not header + body + sidecar + footer + CTA).

## Recommended bias for the build

Build A-v1 as **the conversion ceiling** (Gen-Z's pick, "ships today"). Build B2-v1 as **the taste ceiling** (broader audience, premium voice). The v2s explore what each direction looks like with room to breathe, but the v1s are where the decision should live.

If forced to pre-bias the contact sheet: **B2-v1 is the safest single bet** — it inherits A's witness warmth, sheds B2's editorial tax, and converts both Gen-Z subcultures.
