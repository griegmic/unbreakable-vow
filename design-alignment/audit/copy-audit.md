# Copy Audit: Unbreakable Vow — Contract vs. Monument

## A — Contract: Copy Audit

### Home
**What works:**
- "Make a vow. / *Mean it.*" — stops you cold. Two sentences, opposite energies, the italic flips the tone from performative to serious.
- "Put money on it" in the sub — anchors the financial commitment without jargon. Conversational.
- The chips (Gym 3x, Delete TikTok) are perfectly specific. Witness-testable. Non-cringe.

**What's off:**
- "A contract with yourself" (eyebrow) — kills Gen Z engagement instantly. Feels legal-department. "Contract" undermines the energy that "mean it" just built.
- "$0 vows: when you say the word "contract" to this cohort, the next thing they think about is fine print, not commitment. This pairs the wrong metaphor with the right emotional moment.
- "Tell a friend the thing you keep putting off" — three clauses; could breathe. And "the thing you keep putting off" is passive-voice thinking for an app about action.

**Rewrites:**
- For eyebrow: 
  - *"A vow, made binding."* (cleanest, ties visual metaphor without jargon) **← favorite**
  - "Make it real."
  - "Stake your word."
- For sub:
  - *"Tell a friend what you're committing to. Put money on it. Break it, money goes to charity — or a cause you hate."* (more parallel, more active) **← favorite**
  - "Name a witness. Stake real cash. If you break it, the money's gone — to a cause you believe in, or one you hate."
- For hero label (if A keeps):
  - *"Make a vow. Mean it."* ✓ (already strong)

### Stake
**What works:**
- "Skin in the game." (heading label) — industry shorthand, but *reads* Gen Z because it's borrowed from trading/investing. Not stuffy.
- "Put weight / on your *word*" — the line break + italic hit perfectly. Concrete metaphor, not abstract.
- "Your word, used for good." (charity option) — efficient, hopeful without saccharine.

**What's off:**
- "$25 charged on *seal.* Refunded if you keep your word." — two ideas, one sentence. "Seal" is a jargon moment (users don't know what seal means yet). And "refunded if you keep your word" is backward-looking; should be action-forward.
- "Loss aversion. Maximum motivation." (anti-cause desc) — sounds like a LinkedIn post. Trying too hard to justify the dark-side psychology.
- The "If you break it, money goes to…" label is clunky. Too many words before the choice.

**Rewrites:**
- For pre-CTA:
  - *"$25 held until verdict. Full refund if you keep your word."* **← favorite**
  - "$25 on hold. Refunded when you win."
  - "Charging $25 now. You get it back if you win."
- For loss-aversion desc:
  - *"You hate them. Maximum motivation."* **← favorite**
  - "The stakes cut deeper."
  - "Force yourself to care."
- For section label:
  - *"If broken, goes to…"* (shorter, same meaning)

---

### Witness Landing
**What works:**
- "Joey named you their witness." — specificity + ownership. Witness feels like a role, not an admin task.
- "call it kept or broken." — direct, verb-forward. No metaphor bloat.
- "I'll witness it →" — short, owns the commitment. Works as a CTA.

**What's off:**
- "A vow is asking for you." (summons) — overwritten. Theatrical. Feels like placeholder copy trying to earn its place.
- "Your job: call it kept or broken. / One tap, one Sunday from now." — two separate micro-instructions. Loses rhythm. Second line is filler (they already read the deadline in the doc).
- "Unbreakable Vow turns promises into contracts." (footer explain) — back to contract metaphor. Weak close.

**Rewrites:**
- For summons:
  - *Remove it. Let the headline breathe.* **← favorite**
  - "A vow needs you."
  - "Someone named you witness."
- For role explanation:
  - *"Your job: call it kept or broken. One tap, one Sunday from now."* → *"Your job: call it kept or broken."* (standalone) **← favorite**
  - "You're the ref. One call, one Sunday."
- For footer:
  - *"Unbreakable Vow is a commitment engine with witnesses."* (clearer, less jargon)
  - "What is this? A way to make promises stick. [Learn more]"

## B2 — Monument: Copy Audit

### Home
**What works:**
- "A promise, / made *binding.*" — softer than "contract," same architectural moment. The italic does real work.
- "Vol. I — For people who mean it" (eyebrow) — editorial voice lands perfectly. Magazine sensibility, zero cringe. Implies recurrence without being gimmicky.
- "Be specific. Your witness should know, on sight, whether you kept it." (helper) — *excellent* micro-guidance. Tells you what good looks like without sounding like rules.

**What's off:**
- "Tell a friend the thing you keep putting off. Put money on it. Keep your word, it comes back." — awkward passive structure on money ("it comes back" vs "you get it back"). And "the thing" is weak.
- The examples (Gym three times, Ship the side project) are good, but the "01 / 02 / 03 / 04" numbering system feels more editorial than necessary. Works for magazine design, but copy-wise adds no voice.
- "est. 2026" in masthead — cute but unclear. Establishes what? Authority? Newness? Reads like a design flourish masquerading as copy.

**Rewrites:**
- For sub:
  - *"Tell a friend what you're committing to. Put money on it. Keep your word, and it's yours. Break it, and it goes to charity — or a cause you hate."* (parallel structure, active) **← favorite**
  - "Name it. Stake it. Keep it: your money comes back. Break it: charity, or an enemy gets the cash."
- For helper:
  - ✓ (already excellent)
- For masthead timestamp:
  - *Remove "est. 2026" entirely.* **← favorite** (it's clarifying clutter)
  - "vol. i" (lowercase to lean into magazine feel)

---

### Stake
**What works:**
- "How much is your / *word* worth?" — singular question, huge energy. Makes the decision feel existential, not transactional.
- "Put weight on it" (eyebrow) — echoes home but lands fresh here. Concrete, not abstract.
- The section label structure ("Stake" / "charged on seal · refunded if kept") — inline helper text is brilliant. Saves prose.

**What's off:**
- "$25 held on seal. Returned in full if you keep your word." — "held on seal" is jargon; "keep your word" is past-tense thinking. Should feel forward-motion.
- "a cause you / believe in" and "a cause you / hate" — the line breaks are weird (layout dependency), and "believe in" is softer than the vibe. "Love" would sharpen it.
- "Your word, used for good." vs "Loss aversion. Maximum motivation." — charity line is strong; anti line still sounds like a justification essay.

**Rewrites:**
- For pre-CTA:
  - *"$25 held until verdict. Returned in full if you keep your word."* **← favorite**
  - "$25 held. Full refund when you win."
  - "Locked in now. Yours again when you win."
- For charity option:
  - *"a cause you / love" or "a cause you / believe in"* (keep line break if design allows) — **← favorite** = love
- For loss-aversion:
  - *"You hate it. Maximum motivation."* **← favorite**
  - "Enough to hurt. Enough to win."

---

### Witness Landing
**What works:**
- "Joey named you." (headline) — brutal economy. One sentence, zero decoration. Respect the person.
- "He's made a vow, and you're the one he trusts to call it kept or broken." — *this is exceptional*. It reframes witness role from "you evaluate" to "you matter." Gen Z gold.
- "Call it kept or broken. / No proof uploads, no debate — just your word that what he says is true." — moves from the vow to the witness's role. Specificity on *how* to witness is clarity, not bureaucracy.

**What's off:**
- "Summoned to witness — Apr 21, 2026" (dateline) — "summoned" is formal-legal. Doesn't match the warmth of "Joey named you." And the date is in the meta table below anyway (redundant).
- "Witness Edition" (masthead label) — clarifying, but not copy. Feels UI-ish.
- "What is this? / How Unbreakable Vow works →" — weak link text. "How Unbreakable Vow works" is generic product education. Could be sharper.

**Rewrites:**
- For dateline:
  - *"Apr 21, 2026 — A vow is waiting."* **← favorite**
  - "A witness is needed." 
  - Remove entirely (headline is strong enough) — **← favorite**
- For witness edition label:
  - Remove; keep brand only. Or: *"the witness"* (lowercase, no label)
- For footer link:
  - *"What is this? [Learn how witnessing works →]"* **← favorite** (ties to their role, not generic product)
  - "New to this? [How it works →]"

---

## Head-to-Head

**Winner: B2 (Monument)**, but with conditions. B2's copy assumes emotional maturity and reads *less* transactional. The headline on witness landing — "He's made a vow, and you're the one he trusts to call it kept or broken" — is generational-level insight. It's the sentence that converts skeptics. A's witness copy ("Your job: call it kept or broken. / One tap, one Sunday from now.") is competent but misses the human moment. However, B2 loses points on the home sub (passive "it goes to") and the stake pre-CTA (jargon "held on seal"). A's stake section is tighter overall. If forced to ship one: B2's voice, A's stake micro-copy.

**Breakthrough line worth preserving (either direction):**
- *"He's made a vow, and you're the one he trusts to call it kept or broken."* (B2 witness) — This should live in every direction. It's the line that explains why witnessing matters.

**Lines that must die (one from each):**
- A: *"A contract with yourself"* — jargon kill. Replace with anything about binding/sealing.
- B: *"How much is your word worth?"* — Wait, no. Keep this. Actually: *"Summoned to witness"* dies. It's the one false note.

## For v1 (Tight) and v2 (Loose) Rebuilds

### A — Contract

**v1 (tight):** Cut "A contract with yourself" (eyebrow). Cut "Tell a friend the thing" → replace with "Name your commitment." Cut summons entirely. Cut "one Sunday from now" (redundant with meta). Cut "Unbreakable Vow turns promises into contracts" footer.
  - Result: Snappy, unadorned. All signal.

**v2 (loose):** Keep eyebrow, rewrite as "A vow, made binding." Expand sub to: "Name your commitment. Name a witness. Put money on it. If you break it, the money's gone — to charity, or a cause you hate." Keep summons if designed with restraint. Keep role explanation full. Keep footer but rewrite: "Unbreakable Vow is a commitment engine with real stakes."
  - Result: Room for narrative. Still tight, but breathes.

---

### B2 — Monument

**v1 (tight):** Cut "est. 2026." Rewrite home sub to parallel structure: "Name it. Stake it. Keep it: your money comes back. Break it: charity or your enemy gets paid." Cut "Summoned to witness" dateline. Cut "Witness Edition" label. Tighten "Returned in full if you keep your word" → "Returned if you win."
  - Result: Magazine aesthetic meets efficiency. Editorial, not wordy.

**v2 (loose):** Keep masthead + eyebrow. Expand sub with specificity: "Tell a friend what you're committing to. Put real money behind it. Keep your word: full refund. Break it: money goes to charity you believe in, or a cause you hate." Full role explanation on witness page. Restore dateline (if designed with breathing room). Keep the "He's made a vow…" full sentence structure.
  - Result: Luxe voice. Room for narrative weight. Rewards a re-read.

---

**Final read:** B2 has the better *voice*, but A has more *discipline*. B2's best line (on witnessing) is generational. A's best section (stake) is tighter. Hybrid them if possible. If not: B2 for growth (emotional resonance converts), A as the pairing if budget demands laser focus on conversion.
