## The 5 directions

All 5 solve the same 3 screens (home, stake, witness landing) with the same semantic structure — so a dumb new user reads any of them the same way. What changes is the *voice* of the paper.

| # | Name | DNA in one line | Who this wins | Who it scares off | Gender read |
|---|------|-----------------|---------------|-------------------|-------------|
| **A** | Contract | Fraunces serif, restrained gold, paper-leather. Legal-document DNA. | People who want gravitas. "I mean it." | People who think it looks stuffy / dated. | 55M / 45F |
| **B2** | Modern Monument | Newsreader editorial, brass (not gold), warm charcoal. Aesop × Kinfolk × Standard Hotel. | Design-literate Gen Z of both genders. Taste-coded. | Nobody — it's the broadest center of gravity. | 50/50 |
| **B1** | Financial Instrument | Inter Display + JetBrains Mono, electric green, graphite. Robinhood × Linear × Bloomberg. | Crypto-native, fintech-curious, game-brained. Money-first framing. | Anyone who finds the app cold, tech-bro, or Masc-coded. | 75M / 25F |
| **C** | Blood Oath | Cormorant Unicase stencil + mono, oxblood, bruise-black. Supreme droplist × tattoo flash × typewriter. | Hype-culture Gen Z. Drop energy. "No takebacks" swagger. | People who want the app to feel calm or kind. | 65M / 35F |
| **D2** | Sacred Contemporary | Marcellus + Instrument Sans, rose-gold, warm slate, sage active. Oura × Headspace × Soho House. | Wellness-literate Gen Z of both genders. "Intention" as frame. | People who find it too soft for something binding. | 40M / 60F |

## What I'd pick, if you asked

**Primary: B2 (Modern Monument).** It's the broadest gender appeal, the strongest taste signal, and carries the most product gravitas without leaning into any single subculture. It reads as "premium consumer app that respects you" — which is exactly what turns a 60-second sign-up into a $25 stake. The brass-over-charcoal palette is the safest default that still has teeth.

**Secondary / hybrid candidate: A (Contract).** If we want more "this is a legal document" gravity in the copy/metaphor layer, keep A's document-metaphor on the Witness Landing and Verdict screens, and let B2 drive the home, stake, and marketing surfaces. A is the safest ceiling if B2 ever feels *too* magazine-y and not enough "this binds you."

**Contrarian pick if we want Gen Z virality over taste: C (Blood Oath).** Highest ceiling for organic reach ("ante up," "bleed it," oxblood CTAs). Also highest risk — it signals harder to one subculture. Keep in your back pocket for marketing/campaign art even if we don't ship the core app in it.

**Skip (for core app):** B1 and D2 — both are excellent *as references* but each over-indexes on one gender read. B1 is the best cross-pollination source for data-density and precision (the ledger row on stake, the "position size" framing). D2 is the best cross-pollination source for the softer witness-landing voice ("your part: call it kept or broken") and rounded CTAs.

## How to decide

Look at the contact sheet for 30 seconds. Don't read — just scan. Ask yourself:

1. Which column do you want to show a friend first?
2. Which one would you screenshot and drop in a group chat without adding commentary?
3. Which one makes the stake feel *real* — not a toy?
4. Which one does a 22-year-old woman open without flinching?

If those answers converge on one direction → we lock it.
If they split → tell me which parts of which you want and we build a hybrid.

## What happens after you pick

1. **Manifesto** — I back-rationalize the chosen visual into a design doctrine (voice, typography rules, component DNA, motion principles, "how to decide the next thing" heuristics).
2. **Full screen inventory redesign** — extend the locked direction across every flow (dashboard, refine, seal, certificate, outcome pages, witness verdict).
3. **Prototype + handoff** — Phase 2 / Phase 3 of the original Layer 2 plan.
