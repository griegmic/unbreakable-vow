## What changed since CHECK-IN 2

You picked A and B2. Then a four-person expert panel audited both — copy, product, Gen-Z growth, visual density. The audits agreed more than they disagreed:

- Both directions are shippable. Disagreement is about which audience converts first.
- B2's witness sentence — *"He's made a vow, and you're the one he trusts to call it kept or broken"* — was called "generational-level insight." It now appears in every version.
- Universal kills: `est. 2026`, `Vol. I` (in tight builds), `A contract with yourself`, `Summoned to witness`, `held on seal`, `Witness Edition`, `one Sunday from now`, "Accept" as a witness CTA.
- Universal adds: a third destination option ("charity of their choice" — picked by the witness) to rebalance gender from 65/35 male toward 50/50.

Where they split: **Gen-Z says A ships today; B2 needs polish before it converts on TikTok.** Copy/visual auditors said B2 has the better voice and cleaner type system. Both are true. The v1/v2 split lets you see each direction at two densities.

## What I'd pick

**B2-v1.** It absorbs A's witness warmth (`Joey named you.` — borrowed from A), sheds B2's editorial tax (no Vol. I, no est. 2026, no Summoned), and gets the cleanest type system of the four. Stake is a flat unornamented form (which the product audit said is exactly right for money). Witness landing is the calmest of the four. It looks like the most expensive consumer app on your friends' phones.

**Strong runner-up: A-v1.** Gen-Z said A ships today — and the tight build is even more ready. Stake grid is tight. Witness CTA is `I'm in`. If you want speed of conversion over breadth of audience, A-v1 wins. It also has the most "screenshot bait" energy on the home screen (the gold-on-black hero hits in 800ms).

**Don't pick the v2s as the primary.** They're worth showing for negotiation — "we could go this big if we want the lifestyle play later" — but the v1 builds match the audit feedback better (cognitive load, no decoration). The v2s are the version of each direction you'd ship to a Substack newsletter audience or for a magazine partnership.

## How to decide (the same 4 questions, harder now)

Look at the contact sheet for 60 seconds. Don't read — scan.

1. **Which one would you screenshot to your group chat without commentary?** (Virality test.)
2. **Which one makes the $25 stake feel real but not scary?** (Conversion test.)
3. **Which one would your sister or mom or female cofounder open without flinching?** (Gender test.)
4. **Which one looks like an app made by someone who knows what they're doing?** (Taste test.)

If 3 of 4 land on one column → lock it.
If they split → tell me which parts of which.

## What happens after you pick

1. **Manifesto** — back-rationalize the locked direction into a design doctrine (typography rules, accent rules, motion principles).
2. **Full screen inventory redesign** — extend the locked direction across every screen in the app:
   - Maker flow: home → refine → witness → stake → seal → certificate → live tracking
   - Witness flow: invite landing → vow active view → verdict (kept) → verdict (broken)
   - Outcome pages: vow-kept, vow-broken
   - Dashboard, history, settings (if in scope)
3. **Prototype + handoff** — Phase 2 / Phase 3 of the original Layer 2 plan.
